# Taxonomy 

- **Organizer**  
Represents an organization that is responsible for conducting and managing large scale gathering / events / conferences focused on professional or corporate objectives.

- **Event**  
This refers to an specific instance of large gathering to achieve the objective **Organizer**'s objectives. These can be conferences or Events like *Google I/O 2025*, *North American International Auto Show* etc. They are of 4 types based on **MICE**
    1. **Meetings** : Formal gatherings, often within a single day, where professionals discuss business activities, set goals, or address key challenges. Examples include annual meetings, board meetings, and training sessions.
    2. **Incentives** :Travel rewards provided by companies to employees or partners for excellent performance, often including stays at hotels or resorts.
    3. **Conferences** : Multi-day events where a large group of people share knowledge, often featuring guest speakers and attracting a wider audience.
    4. **Exhibitions** : Trade shows where organizations showcase their products and services, facilitating networking and business development. 


- **Engagement**  
This will refer to a specific activity within an **Event** like a speaker keynote session, presentations from speakers, exhibitions, booths, breakouts etc. 

- **Registration ID**  
This will refer to a set of unique identifers that the **Organizer** will issue for a specific **Event**. These identifiers will be used to provide participation for people with different goals like Attendees, Presentors, Exhibitors etc.

- **Attendee**   
Participants who come to learn, network, or evaluate a product etc.

- **Presentor**   
Individuals delivering talks, participating in panels, or leading workshops.

- **Exhibitor**  
Companies (sending individuals from their company) or individuals showcasing products/services.

 - **Staff**  
People running the event operationally or programmatically.
